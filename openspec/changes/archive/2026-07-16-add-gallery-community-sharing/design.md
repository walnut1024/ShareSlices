# Gallery community sharing design

## Context

ShareSlices currently has one public access channel: an Artifact-scoped Share link resolves the latest accessible Publication and serves its selected immutable Version. Owner management, Preview, Export, thumbnails, the CLI, and the official Skill all assume User ownership; there is no anonymous discovery read model, public Creator identity, cross-User copy operation, or moderation lifecycle. Existing `ContentBundle` identity and deletion boundaries are deliberately User-scoped.

Gallery introduces a second public channel with different semantics. A Creator intentionally lists one fixed ready Version for anonymous discovery, while Share with link continues to control a time-bounded Publication independently. Public discovery expands the threat and governance surface of uploaded HTML and JavaScript, so Gallery cannot reuse compatibility deployments where untrusted content shares the management Origin or credential boundary.

This change crosses Web, API, PostgreSQL, Worker, private object storage, deployment validation, CLI, Agent protocol, and the official Skill. `PRODUCT.md` and `CONTEXT.md` own the durable product and vocabulary decisions; ADR 0007 owns channel separation and fixed-Version identity, ADR 0008 owns the Gallery isolation gate, and ADR 0005 continues to forbid cross-User Content-bundle reuse.

## Goals / Non-Goals

**Goals:**

- Create an independently authorized, fixed-Version Gallery listing with explicit update and permanent withdrawal.
- Provide safe anonymous discovery, trusted metadata pages, Creator profiles, Download, and signed-in Save a copy.
- Preserve independent ownership, quota, deletion, provenance, and idempotency across copied Artifacts.
- Add permission acceptance, safety checks, reports, review, Removal, Artifact takedown, restrictions, Appeals, notification, and auditable administration.
- Refuse Gallery enablement unless deployment topology and browser response policy provide the required untrusted-content boundary.
- Preserve existing Share links, Publications, CLI compatibility, and unlisted Artifacts through an additive migration.

**Non-Goals:**

- Mobile or tablet support, responsive Artifact adaptation, likes, comments, follows, collections, private messages, or algorithmic feeds.
- Paid placement, custom license selection, organization or private Gallery, batch Gallery sharing, or restoration of a withdrawn listing.
- Cross-User Content-bundle aliasing, mutation of a copied Artifact when its source changes, or technical recall of completed downloads.
- Public Gallery browse, copy, download, or report commands in the first CLI and Skill release.
- Sanitizing arbitrary Artifact code into trusted code or claiming that deterministic checks prove content harmless.

## Decisions

### Store Gallery identity separately from Publication

Add a `gallery_listing` aggregate keyed by an internal ID and never-reused opaque slug. It references the owned Artifact, Creator profile, lifecycle status, current public revision when one exists, concurrency revision, timestamps, and closure evidence. Immutable `gallery_listing_revision` records bind one ready Version, metadata, grant acceptance, and cover identity. An optional non-public update proposal records its base concurrency revision, candidate listing revision, safety result, and review state without replacing the current public revision. A partial uniqueness constraint over open listings enforces at most one Pending or Listed listing per Artifact. Restricted is the listing projection of an Artifact-level Public-sharing restriction, not a second restriction authority.

Share, update, and withdraw execute through an Artifact-scoped transaction and idempotency scope that stores a normalized input fingerprint and rejects same-key, different-input reuse. A replay of already accepted equivalent input reads the immutable historical operation outcome and a separately labeled current resource projection even after Gallery disablement or a later governance block; this recovery path performs no new admission or mutation and never presents a stale successful URL or lifecycle as current. An initial share keeps the listing Pending until its candidate passes or is accepted through review. Policy or review rejection closes that never-public listing as Removed with `initial_policy_rejection`; it offers correction and a later fresh share but no Appeal. A governance block closes it with `initial_governance_block` and permits a fresh share only after the block no longer remains in force. Both permanently keep the unexposed slug non-public. Update requires the caller's expected concurrency revision, stages one candidate, and atomically promotes the complete candidate only after required checks and review. A Listed current revision remains committed while the candidate is pending and continues serving only while effective access remains eligible. An active Public-sharing restriction, Artifact takedown, Administrator Removal still in force, or pending Appeal blocks Owner update submission and promotion. Applying one while an update proposal is open closes only that proposal without changing the committed revision, and a later update requires a fresh proposal after the block is reversed or cleared.

Each listing has a nullable current closure-reason projection: `creator_withdrawal`, `artifact_deleted`, `account_deleted`, `initial_policy_rejection`, `initial_governance_block`, or `administrator_removal`. Creator withdrawal moves a Pending or Listed listing to Withdrawn. Artifact or account deletion moves a Pending, Listed, or previously public `administrator_removal` listing to Withdrawn; a never-public Removed listing stays Removed and records source deletion. Initial rejection, initial governance block, and Administrator Removal produce Removed. Every lifecycle transition compares and advances the listing revision. Competing closure attempts from the same base revision serialize; the first commit wins and every loser returns the resulting current projection without overwriting lifecycle, closure reason, or public-response behavior. Only the explicit later Artifact- or account-deletion conversion of a previously public `administrator_removal` can replace a terminal projection. Every close and restore appends immutable lifecycle history. Only a previously public listing closed with `administrator_removal` can be restored, and only when no replacement has ever been created; restoration clears the current reason without erasing history. Deleting the Artifact or account later converts that previously public Removed listing to Withdrawn with the deletion reason and permanently ends restoration. A never-public Removed listing stays `404` and cannot restore or re-share without a source. Withdraw or Removal closes every pending candidate with the listing. Withdraw retires the slug permanently. Re-sharing after withdrawal creates a new listing and slug rather than reviving a closed address.

This is preferred to reusing `artifact_publication` because Publication follows link availability and expiration, while Gallery pins a Version and has governance, discovery, grant, and permanent-withdrawal semantics. It is preferred to indexing Share links because that would make link and Gallery actions produce hidden cross-channel side effects.

### Separate the trusted Gallery page from Artifact execution

The Web Origin owns `/gallery`, `/gallery/{slug}`, and `/creators/{slug}` metadata pages, search, trusted controls, and account-aware actions. The trusted API atomically authorizes and issues a short-lived opaque player credential bound to `(listing identity, committed listing revision, fixed Version)` and records its listing view once. Reusing the credential or requesting assets never records another view. The listing page embeds the bound entry URL from the configured Untrusted-content origin, and every relative asset URL carries the same binding. An update causes new authorizations to bind the new revision, while an existing authorization can finish loading only its bound Version until its short expiry and only while the listing remains effectively accessible and Gallery remains enabled and eligible. Gallery disablement, Withdrawal, Removal, restriction, takedown, or deployment ineligibility blocks subsequent requests immediately.

An authenticated governance API can issue a different short-lived Administrator review credential bound to `(Administrator, case or proposal, candidate Version)`. Every review entry and asset request revalidates active Administrator authority and an open reviewable case or proposal in addition to the credential tuple. Role revocation or case closure blocks later requests immediately. The isolated content runtime applies the same manifest, sandbox, network, storage, cache, referrer, and logging restrictions but records no public view and accepts that credential on no public route. If the isolated review runtime or one of its own security dependencies is unavailable, the administration surface shows only static evidence and explicit preview unavailability; it never executes suspicious candidate content on the trusted Web or management Origin.

The Untrusted-content service is a separate content-only Hono application entrypoint, listener, deployment, and ingress in the API codebase. Its dependency graph contains only route-disjoint public-player and Administrator-review credential validation, public effective-access or case-bound candidate lookup, manifest lookup, private-object streaming, policy headers, health, and telemetry. Public credentials cannot authorize review routes and review credentials cannot authorize public routes. It is never mounted through the management `buildApp`, and it has no Better Auth, management routes, aggregate writer, credentialed CORS, or mutation adapter. The content Origin sends no management credentials and sets no content-site cookies.

The iframe uses `sandbox="allow-scripts"` without `allow-same-origin` or Fullscreen API authority. A trusted parent control uses its own Viewer activation to request Full screen for the player or iframe; Artifact code cannot initiate that platform action. Document responses also apply Content Security Policy sandbox restrictions so the same capability boundary survives direct top-level navigation to a content URL. Artifact documents therefore have opaque Origins and receive no persistent local storage, IndexedDB, Cache Storage, Service Worker, or state shared across listings. Player-authorization, entry, and asset responses use `Cache-Control: no-store` and `Referrer-Policy: no-referrer`, and every log path redacts the player credential. Response policy permits CORS without credentials only for revision-scoped manifest files, including packaged module scripts and relative data requests. External networking, forms, popups, top-level navigation, automatic downloads, clipboard, camera, microphone, location, and other powerful capabilities remain blocked. Download, Save a copy, Report, Full screen, and navigation execute only in the trusted page. The Untrusted-content origin must also use a different browser registrable site from the Web and API; sibling subdomains remain ineligible even when management cookies are currently host-only.

A deployment eligibility module and deployment checker consume the same policy contract and fixtures derived from the actual Web, API, Untrusted-content, site, cookie, and network configuration. A shared runtime gate also consumes live readiness for the current grant, production challenge verifier, Administrator authority, reporting, notification, Appeal, governance, and isolated-content capabilities before every public entrypoint and new expanding operation. Configuration, redeployment, or runtime health loss transitions the gate to ineligible and makes public routes return pre-lookup `503`; recovery requires every gate again and does not require a redeploy. The topology check fails closed when the content Origin shares the management Origin, registrable site, or credential boundary. Gallery is disabled by default and no force flag bypasses an ineligible result. Reusing the current Viewer Origin is allowed only if it independently satisfies this validator; otherwise deployment supplies a separate content Origin.

Public response precedence is deterministic: Gallery disablement or ineligibility returns generic `503` before listing lookup; otherwise Withdrawn returns generic `410` even when a temporary block also exists; an effectively accessible Listed revision returns `200`; and every other public listing state returns generic `404`. These responses expose no listing or governance metadata. Authenticated recovery of an already accepted operation is a non-public operation read and does not grant new listing access.

This is preferred to warnings on shared-host deployments because browser trust boundaries cannot be repaired by UI copy. It is preferred to disabling JavaScript because interactive agent-generated Artifacts are a core product format.

### Build a dedicated anonymous discovery read path

Public listing and Creator-profile reads use an explicit projection that contains only approved metadata, active status, review eligibility, cover identity, opaque URLs, and deterministic ordering keys. It never joins email, authentication methods, management identifiers, private Artifact names, object keys, or raw governance evidence into public responses. User-controlled title, description, tags, display name, and biography remain bounded plain text rendered with escaping; avatar identity resolves only to a platform-managed safe raster asset or neutral placeholder, never an arbitrary remote or executable resource.

Featured is an auditable administrative position ordered by that position and a stable listing-identity tie-breaker. Ineligibility durably removes the placement, and later clearance does not restore it without a new administrative action. Newest, the default index, search, exact-tag, and Creator collections order by immutable listing creation time descending and stable listing identity; search matches normalized fields but does not introduce a changing relevance score. Exact tag filters and opaque cursor pagination keep traversal deterministic. Cards use only a static Version-specific cover and never embed executable content.

The first share requires explicit confirmation of a public Creator display name and may stage an optional platform-managed avatar and biography. It can prefill only an existing non-email public profile value. A per-User uniqueness constraint and profile concurrency revision serialize first-share staging before proposal acceptance: equivalent normalized fields can reuse the one staged profile, while conflicting fields return a revision conflict without another profile or proposal. Profile PATCH uses the same revision rule. The profile remains outside public projections until the first listing revision is promoted; a Pending or rejected initial proposal exposes no Creator profile. Authenticated profile management can later update those public fields without changing listing identity, while search uses the current display name.

Public metadata routes become indexable only after reporting and takedown are operational. Artifact content responses remain `noindex` so search results land on the trusted listing page.

### Keep cover work non-blocking and Version-specific

Gallery listings reference a cover derived from their fixed Version and renderer revision, not the Owner-facing latest-ready Artifact thumbnail. Listing activation does not wait for the cover; the public projection returns a neutral placeholder until the existing isolated rendering pipeline produces a result. Cover failure is terminal for that render attempt but does not change listing or Version state.

Reusing the thumbnail rendering boundary is preferred to synchronous capture because publication readiness and existing thumbnail policy already establish that rendering failure must not block content availability. The Gallery identity remains Version-specific even when the underlying Content bundle and renderer output can be reused within the same User boundary.

### Create copies through a recoverable Server-side job

Save a copy authorizes a publicly accessible Listed revision and, in one acceptance transaction, binds its immutable listing revision and fixed-Version snapshot, consumes a per-Copier account rate-limit unit, reserves the Copier's Artifact and storage quotas, acquires one durable source-retention reference, and creates a durable copy job before returning an accepted outcome. Source deletion serializes with that boundary: deletion first leaves no operation or reference, while acceptance first retains the fixed source objects until the job reaches a proven terminal state. New or unknown-key acceptance uses the current public listing boundary. Authenticated replay of the same accepted idempotency key and normalized input reads the prior durable operation even after source closure or Gallery ineligibility, without another listing authorization, rate-limit unit, reservation, or source access; reusing the key for different input is a conflict, while another key may intentionally create another independent copy within those controls. The Worker copies the fixed Version's committed manifest and assets into the Copier's ownership boundary, commits an independently owned Content bundle and ready Version, and records immediate-source, root-source, and root-Creator provenance. Within the Copier's ownership boundary, existing same-User content reuse may apply; the operation never exposes or aliases the source User's bundle identity.

The intake model gains an explicit source kind for Server-originated Gallery copy so the resulting Version retains durable provenance without pretending a browser uploaded a raw archive. Copy retries and reconciliation use one idempotency key and job identity, clean abandoned destination objects, and never expose a partial bundle. Ready commit converts reserved quota to usage; proven failure or cancellation releases it after cleanup; an indeterminate operation holds its reservation until reconciliation establishes a terminal result. The resulting Artifact defaults to the Gallery title, remains unshared and unlisted, and can be renamed normally.

Source events after acceptance follow one fixed race policy:

| Source event before copy is ready | Non-terminal job outcome | Source retention and later governance |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Owner update or withdrawal | Continue against the accepted snapshot | Never read the replacement revision |
| Artifact deletion or distinct source Creator account deletion | Continue against the accepted snapshot | Hold one durable source reference per job until terminal; expose retained objects through no route |
| Administrator Removal, Artifact takedown, Public-sharing restriction, Gallery disablement, or deployment ineligibility | Serialize against ready commit; cancel if the block wins | Clean partial destination objects and release quota and source reference |
| Ready commit wins a governance race | Keep the completed independent copy | Apply content-level propagation afterward through immutable provenance |
| Copier-account deletion | Serialize against ready commit; cancel if deletion wins | If ready wins, account deletion includes the committed copy; never leave an ownerless resource |

When source Creator and Copier are the same account, the Copier-account deletion row takes precedence over source-account continuation. Cancelled is a durable terminal copy outcome and cannot retry to ready. Each ready, failed, or cancelled terminal reconciliation releases its durable source-retention reference exactly once; indeterminate work keeps the reference, and the last release resumes source-object cleanup. Direct and descendant Gallery-saved copies match content-level governance through immutable provenance rooted at the governed listing and fixed Version. Equivalent independent Uploads do not match. Each propagated restriction basis references the source decision that created it. Reversing that source decision closes only its derived bases, resolves Appeals against them as reversed or moot, and recomputes each affected Artifact while preserving unrelated governance bases. A later independent Version does not silently clear an active restriction.

This is preferred to browser Download followed by Upload because that loses atomic provenance, honest progress, quota preflight, and recoverability. Cross-User bundle aliasing remains rejected because it would reopen the existence side channel and shared deletion boundary settled by ADR 0005.

### Stream Download from the fixed committed Version

Anonymous Download reuses the existing manifest-authorized ZIP assembly behind a new effective-access authorization boundary. Authorization fixes the immutable Version and acquires a bounded active-download source-read lease before streaming begins. Later closure, restriction, Artifact deletion, or account deletion blocks new requests but does not interrupt an already authorized stream. The lease retains only the fixed Version's committed objects until finish, abort, or maximum duration, releases exactly once, and lets deletion cleanup resume after the last hold. The ZIP contains only normalized Artifact files and no injected attribution asset, while the trusted page and response metadata expose source and grant information.

The public endpoint never exposes raw uploads, staging data, object-storage locations, or another Version. Rate limits and request evidence are independent from Creator product quotas. ZIP assembly uses instance and deployment concurrency ceilings, bounded buffers with streaming back-pressure, a maximum processing duration, and abort cleanup that releases readers and temporary resources when the client disconnects.

### Model lifecycle and review as orthogonal state

Listing lifecycle uses Pending, Listed, Withdrawn, and Removed. Review status uses Clear, Reviewing, and Restricted. An active direct Artifact takedown contributes a Reviewing basis and blocks effective access; any Artifact-level Public-sharing restriction takes review-status precedence as Restricted. Reversal closes only the applicable basis and recomputes status from every remaining report, takedown, restriction, and proposal review. Deterministic safety work evaluates an immutable candidate revision under a checked `GallerySafetyPolicy`. Each policy version fixes input bounds, stable finding and reason codes, pass, reject, or review mapping, evidence-digest format, and replay rules. A policy upgrade records a new evaluation and never rewrites old evidence. The Worker computes findings and a mapped result; the API snapshots the policy version, owns every transition, and prevents stale results from promoting a proposal.

Ordinary content is eligible to proceed, a clear policy violation is rejected, and a suspicious initial candidate keeps its listing Pending and Reviewing. A safety pass is proposal evidence, not authority to clear an independent review or governance state. A suspicious update proposal remains non-public and Reviewing while the existing current revision and its listing state remain unchanged. A report can mark a current Listed item Reviewing without automatically hiding it; an Administrator or high-confidence signal can apply a Public-sharing restriction while evidence is reviewed. Clear is available only when no open review basis or restriction remains.

`gallery_report`, `gallery_decision`, and `gallery_appeal` records preserve typed reasons, sanitized reporter evidence, actor identity where authenticated, request identifiers, timestamps, and decision history. An approved versioned `GalleryAppealPolicy` defines appealable decision categories and window duration. Each appealable decision atomically snapshots its policy version and computes a Server-time deadline; later policy versions affect only later decisions. A direct reversal after Appeal acceptance atomically resolves that Appeal as reversed or moot and recomputes every dependent block. Report acceptance atomically binds only a publicly accessible Listed current revision and creates a minimum private evidence snapshot and object-retention hold. Artifact deletion removes management and public access but preserves that evidence until the case and every accepted Appeal are terminal, every applicable snapshotted Appeal deadline has passed, and the approved governance-retention deadline has passed. A non-appealable terminal case has no Appeal-deadline condition, while an accepted Appeal that outlives its submission deadline keeps the hold until it reaches a terminal result. Report detail, Appeal explanation, administrative rationale, and notification text are bounded escaped plain text with no markup execution, automatic link creation, or remote-resource retrieval. Anonymous reports require a challenge and rate limit. Public projections never expose reporter identity or raw evidence.

The API owns safety policy and state transitions, while a recoverable Worker job inspects committed candidate content and returns one evidenced pass, reject, or review result. The same candidate and job identity collapse retries and cannot promote a stale proposal. Anonymous-report verification uses a `ChallengeVerifier` application interface with a configured production adapter and test adapter; Gallery eligibility requires the production verifier to be operational, while the provider vendor remains deployment configuration rather than product policy.

Gallery administration introduces an explicit scoped Administrator authorization checked before queue reads, decisions, Appeals, and Featured changes. Role assignment and use are audited and do not grant Artifact ownership. Material listing decisions create durable in-product notifications for the affected Creator; Artifact takedown and restriction decisions notify the affected Owner even when that User has never had a Creator profile. Both can use the applicable Appeal path. Email delivery is outside the first release.

Remove from Gallery closes only the listing with `administrator_removal`. Its restoration transaction requires a previously public committed revision and rechecks the one-active-listing constraint; once any replacement exists, the older Removed listing cannot be restored. A never-public initial rejection or governance block cannot restore its slug. Artifact takedown blocks both Gallery and Share-link effective public access while retaining the independent Publication lifecycle, Link sharing status, Owner management, and review evidence. Content-level takedown can place provenance-matching direct and descendant saved copies under Public-sharing restriction through a privileged internal lookup; this lookup excludes unrelated independent Uploads, is never exposed as a User-facing content-existence oracle, and never silently deletes private copies. Each derived restriction records its source decision so reversal closes only matching derived bases, resolves Appeals against those bases, and recomputes copies without clearing unrelated restrictions or review work.

This separation is preferred to one overloaded status because Creator withdrawal, platform Removal, temporary review, and Artifact-wide takedown have different reversibility, notification, and serving effects.

### Check cross-runtime job contracts

Cover, safety, and copy work cross the TypeScript API, PostgreSQL, Rust Worker, and private object storage through one checked, versioned, language-neutral contract. Every job records a contract version, immutable input identity and snapshot, state, attempt and fenced lease, retry policy, result or stable failure codes, object-layout revision, timestamps, and trace identifiers. The API owns enqueue, cancellation, policy snapshot, quota reservation, and product-state transition. The Worker owns only fenced claim and attempt evidence, computed findings or object results, heartbeat, and terminal execution result. A stale lease or incompatible contract version cannot commit output.

| Job | Immutable input | Execution states and terminal result | Product commit owner |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Cover | Listing candidate, fixed Version, renderer revision | Queued, processing; ready or failed | API attaches only a matching result to the committed revision |
| Safety | Proposal, candidate Version, `GallerySafetyPolicy` version | Queued, processing; pass, reject, review, or failed | API maps current evidence to proposal and governance transitions |
| Copy | Operation, Copier, accepted listing revision, fixed Version, provenance, quota reservation | Accepted, processing; ready, failed, or cancelled, with indeterminate reconciliation evidence | API atomically commits resources and quota or cancels and releases them |

The checked schema defines additive compatibility rules and N/N-1 read fixtures so a rolling API and Worker deployment either interoperate or fail readiness before claiming unsupported jobs. Object layouts use immutable attempt-scoped destinations; only a fenced terminal commit can make an output authoritative, and reconciliation removes abandoned attempts.

### Version permission acceptance and provenance

Each accepted listing proposal stores the exact Gallery permission-grant revision, acceptance time, accepting User, and fixed Version as immutable evidence. Promotion installs that proposal-bound reference as the listing's current accepted grant. Update requires acceptance for a replacement Version and also for a metadata-only update when current policy requires renewal at the next Share or Update proposal. Proposal acceptance snapshots the applicable grant policy; a later grant change applies to the next Share or Update proposal and neither rewrites nor strands the open proposal. Gallery view, withdrawal, and accepted-operation recovery remain exempt. If no current grant is configured, read-only state preserves any listing and historical acceptance but reports stable no-current-grant unavailability and admits no share or update proposal.

Source attribution records the immediate listing and Version plus the root listing, root Version, and original Creator. Re-sharing a copied Artifact displays the current Creator and the original Creator while keeping lineage identifiers private. If the original account is deleted, public attribution becomes `Original Creator unavailable`; email and authentication identity never enter provenance.

The product state and evidence boundaries are fixed by this design. Exact legal text, the initial approved `GalleryAppealPolicy` duration, and jurisdiction-specific notice and retention approval remain launch dependencies rather than unresolved product-state choices.

### Extend the CLI and Skill additively

Human and Agent CLI surfaces add `artifact gallery view`, `artifact gallery share`, `artifact gallery update`, and `artifact gallery withdraw`. Capability discovery adds canonical Agent operation IDs for those four operations, and every outcome uses the existing envelope, resource-evidence, uncertainty, and next-action rules. Read-only Gallery state includes configured current permission-grant evidence even before a listing exists, or stable no-current-grant unavailability plus any historical acceptance. Agent protocol adds an explicit permission-acceptance action distinct from irreversible confirmation. Gallery share and update require complete Version, metadata, profile when first needed, and grant inputs; withdraw is explicit and irreversible because it retires the URL. A replacement after reversed Administrator Removal also requires irreversible confirmation because it permanently forfeits restoration of the old listing identity, URL, and counters.

The official Skill routes explicit Gallery intent only when the installed CLI advertises the required operation and mutually supported Agent protocol. It never widens Upload or link-sharing intent into Gallery discovery and never falls back to REST or human output. Public browsing and interactions remain Web-only in this release.

### Bound observability and privacy

Operational events record stable listing, copy-job, report, decision, and request identifiers without raw Artifact content, credentials, reporter secrets, or public slugs in logs. Successful content, Download, and ready-copy boundaries update non-identifying listing aggregates; update promotion preserves them and a replacement listing starts from zero. Public counters are not shown or used for ranking. Pseudonymous raw signals needed for rate limiting and abuse investigation expire within 30 days; longer-lived aggregates are non-identifying.

Creators receive operation results and durable in-product material governance notifications through authenticated management, not one notification per view, copy, or download. Administrative Featured actions and all governance decisions are auditable.

## Risks / Trade-offs

- **[Risk] Publicly discoverable code can attack management sessions or Viewers.** → Disable Gallery unless topology validation proves the Untrusted-content boundary, then enforce manifest-only routing, sandbox controls, response policy, and trusted controls.
- **[Risk] The self-contained network policy breaks Artifacts that depend on remote APIs or assets.** → Reject that dependency for Gallery, explain the self-contained requirement, and preserve ordinary link-sharing compatibility separately.
- **[Risk] A listing update can bypass prior review by swapping content.** → Stage an immutable non-public candidate, rerun safety checks and grant acceptance, promote only the expected proposal atomically, and keep the prior Version committed until promotion and publicly active only while still eligible.
- **[Risk] Cross-User copying can leak content identity or create shared deletion state.** → Authorize only through a publicly accessible Listed revision, copy into the recipient boundary, allow reuse only within that recipient, and keep internal moderation matching privileged.
- **[Risk] Copy jobs or ZIP streams can amplify storage and bandwidth cost.** → Preflight and reserve quotas, rate-limit copies per account and public Downloads per source, use bounded jobs and concurrency, reconcile abandoned objects, and keep costs off the Creator's product quota.
- **[Risk] Withdrawal cannot recall completed copies or downloads.** → State this in the permission grant and confirmation UI, block only future access, and preserve independent ownership semantics.
- **[Risk] Reports can be abused to censor content or expose private evidence.** → Rate-limit anonymous reports, do not auto-remove ordinary reports, separate review from lifecycle, hide reporter identity, and provide auditable Appeals.
- **[Trade-off] Permanent URL retirement loses prior counters on re-share.** → Preserve the stronger withdrawal guarantee and internal audit while starting the replacement with a new listing identity and URL.
- **[Trade-off] Gallery remains unavailable on shared-host and mobile compatibility deployments.** → Prefer a defensible security and support boundary over a feature that silently exceeds the deployment's real isolation.

## Migration Plan

1. Add nullable and additive Gallery, immutable listing-revision proposal, profile, copy-job, grant, governance, provenance, engagement-aggregate, bounded Download-lease, and tombstone schema plus checked cross-runtime job contracts, repository support, and reconciliation while Gallery remains disabled.
2. Add owner listing and profile contracts, the recoverable safety Worker, versioned `GallerySafetyPolicy`, scoped Administrator authorization, challenge verification, governance administration, and the deployment eligibility validator. Contract tests keep public routes unavailable while disabled or ineligible.
3. Add the trusted discovery API and Web routes and the independent content-only Untrusted-content application, then deploy it on a separate browser registrable site and verify that sibling-subdomain, shared-host, credential-spanning, and management-route configurations fail validation.
4. Add Save a copy, Download, cover projection, reporting, in-product notifications, account and anonymous rate limits, listing engagement aggregates, and privacy retention jobs.
5. Add Web management labels, Creator profile management, and Gallery actions, then add CLI, Agent protocol, and official Skill support with compatibility tests.
6. Enable Gallery only after the approved terms and notices, initial `GallerySafetyPolicy`, Administrator bootstrap and revocation, production challenge adapter, reporting, takedown, Appeals, and all security gates pass. Existing Artifacts remain unlisted and existing Publications remain unchanged.

Rollback first fences new Gallery admission and Worker claims, then makes public Gallery entrypoints return generic `503` before resource lookup. Queued or leased cover and safety jobs pause or requeue with their immutable inputs; lease fencing prevents a stale Worker from attaching a result after disablement. Accepted or processing copy jobs cancel or reconcile to a proven terminal result, clean partial attempt objects, and release quota reservations and source references exactly once. Indeterminate copies keep both holds until reconciliation proves ready, failed, or cancelled, and the last source release resumes deleted-source cleanup. Already authorized Download streams keep their bounded source-read leases until finish, abort, or maximum-duration expiry, then release exactly once. No disabled Worker is ready while incompatible work remains claimable.

Listing, grant, profile, and governance data remain durable so a later eligible enablement can resume without migration reversal. Existing Share links continue through the Publication path. Already authorized Download streams may finish, and completed independent copies and downloads cannot be rolled back; no new Gallery-authorized access begins while disabled.

## Launch dependencies

Product, lifecycle, security, governance, migration, and client-surface decisions are resolved. Release enablement still depends on four concrete operational inputs:

- Approved exact permission-grant, public notice, Appeal notice, versioned `GalleryAppealPolicy`, and retention text for the enabled jurisdictions.
- A configured production `ChallengeVerifier` adapter with readiness and failure-mode tests.
- A secure initial Administrator bootstrap and revocation procedure with audited role assignment.
- A reviewed initial `GallerySafetyPolicy` version with stable findings, mappings, evidence, fixtures, and rollout approval.

Gallery remains disabled until each dependency is supplied and every eligibility gate passes.
